About

I build quantitative analyses for high-stakes technology questions where economics, engineering constraints, and policy decisions interact. My goal is to produce work that is technically grounded and immediately useful to academic and policy audiences.

My professional background spans data science, software systems, and commercialization strategy, which informs an implementation-aware view of AI governance and industrial policy.

Research Agenda

Compute Bottlenecks and Cost Dynamics

Modeling how chip supply, advanced packaging, and data-center capacity shape the pace and distribution of frontier model development.

Power Systems and AI Growth

Quantifying energy constraints on model training and inference, with attention to grid-level and regional policy implications.

Market Design for AI Access

Applying game-theoretic and mechanism-design reasoning to allocation, competition, and governance in scarce compute environments.

Current Projects

Chips, Power, and Policy: How Frontier AI Compute Constraints Shift from Silicon to Energy

Quantitative working paper mapping the shift from semiconductor bottlenecks to power and infrastructure constraints in frontier AI scaling.

The project links industrial organization, supply-chain frictions, and policy options for resilient AI ecosystem planning.

Applied Forecasting for Resource Planning

Developing demand and risk models that can be adapted to compute capacity planning and public-interest infrastructure decisions.

Evidence Design for Policy Communication

Translating technical model outputs into decision-ready narratives for non-technical stakeholders.
